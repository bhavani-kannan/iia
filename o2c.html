Where the Process Breaks Today

Eight friction points across the O2C chain that automation directly addresses. Each represents a stage where manual effort, disconnected systems or reactive behaviour creates cost and risk.

01 Credit Management
Credit decisions are static and manually governed
Credit limits are static with no adjustment for actual payment behaviour. Holds are managed manually with no risk scoring, delaying good orders and missing bad risk.
02 Sales Planning
Regional targets locked in Excel
Sales targets live in Excel and are reconciled against SAP actuals manually at month end. No live variance view or automated alert when a region drifts from plan.
03 Order Management
Order exceptions handled reactively
Stuck orders need manual triage across teams to diagnose and resolve. No prioritisation by revenue at risk or SLA deadline - every exception looks the same.
04 Delivery Tracking
Live delivery data invisible to the business
Live delivery status sits in the third-party tracker with no feed into SAP. Teams react to delays after the fact - never before the customer is impacted.
05 Invoice Quality
Billing errors create rework before cash is even collected
Pricing mismatches and billing errors are not caught before invoice dispatch. At 5-10% error rates on complex orders, each correction extends DSO and feeds the disputes queue.
06 Collections
Collections driven by aging reports, not risk
Collections run from static aging reports with no scoring by default probability. High-risk accounts get the same attention as low-risk ones.
07 Deductions and Disputes
Customer deductions managed on spreadsheets
Customer deductions are tracked manually on spreadsheets with weeks-long resolution cycles. At 2-4% of sales, the revenue leakage is material but largely invisible to the business.
08 Cash Application
Manual payment matching creates close delays
Incoming payments are matched to open invoices manually in SAP FI. Partial payments and reference errors generate exception volumes that delay the monthly close.

Why a full deployment is required here

O2C intelligence spans three disconnected source systems - SAP, a third-party tracker and Excel. Unlike a single-system agent deployment, cross-stage insight (linking a delivery delay to AR risk to collections priority) requires a shared data foundation. The six-layer approach is not overhead here - it is what makes the agents coherent across the process chain.

O2C Process Architecture

Six agents run in parallel across the enterprise O2C portfolio - not as sequential steps, but as independent capabilities each operating on their own data domain and trigger. At enterprise scale, orders, deliveries, invoices and payments are all active simultaneously. The agents work across all of them, all the time.

Six agents run in parallel across the enterprise O2C portfolio. On any given day, orders are being placed, shipments are in transit, invoices are outstanding and payments are arriving - all at once. O2C Intelligence Pod

Three tiers of intelligence applied across the O2C chain. Predictive models surface risk and variance before they become problems. Prescriptive recommendations tell teams what to do about them. Agentic automation removes the manual effort entirely where human oversight is not required.

PRESCRIPTIVE

Delivery Delay Prediction
Models live carrier signals, route history and shipment data from the third-party tracker to score each in-transit order on delay probability. Risk flags are generated continuously, giving logistics teams time to act before the customer is impacted.
Business value: Proactive intervention on at-risk shipments reduces late delivery claims, customer penalties and reactive firefighting.
Order Exception Prioritisation
Ranks all stuck orders in SAP SD by revenue at risk, SLA proximity and days blocked. Each exception surfaces with a recommended resolution action so teams direct effort where the business impact is highest.
Business value: Exception queues are always ordered by business impact. No high-value order waits while low-priority ones are resolved.
Order Exception Agent
Monitors SAP SD continuously for stuck orders, diagnoses root cause across credit, inventory and pricing signals, and drafts resolution steps routed to the responsible owner for approval. No action is taken without human sign-off.
Business value: Every stuck order is seen, diagnosed and actioned with a full audit trail - without manual triage effort from the team.
AR Default Risk Scoring
Scores all open invoices daily by late payment and default probability using payment history, invoice age, dispute patterns and external signals. Risk-ranked output replaces the static aging report as the collections team's working view.
Business value: Collections effort concentrates on the accounts that need attention today, not the ones that happen to be oldest on the list.
Collections Outreach Recommendations
Prescribes the right action per invoice - automated reminder, direct follow-up or dispute escalation - based on risk score, account value and relationship history. Recommended outreach is drafted and queued for controller review before sending.
Business value: Collections strategy is consistent and evidence-based across every account, not shaped by individual judgement or bandwidth.
Credit Risk Agent
Monitors payment behaviour and AR signals continuously across the customer base, scoring each account on default probability as conditions change. When a threshold is breached, a limit adjustment or hold recommendation is surfaced to the credit controller for approval before any change is applied.
Business value: Credit exposure is managed on live signals rather than periodic reviews. At-risk accounts are identified before an order is placed, not after it ships.
Predictive Cash Forecasting
Builds 30, 60 and 90-day cash receipt forecasts from live AR aging, customer payment behaviour and open order pipeline. Forecasts refresh automatically as payment events and order status change across the chain.
Business value: Finance leadership plans liquidity and working capital deployment on a granular, behaviour-grounded forecast updated in near real time.
Working Capital Optimisation
Surfaces the highest-impact levers across DSO reduction, collections timing and credit policy, each quantified by expected cash release and ranked by implementation effort. Recommendations refresh as AR conditions and payment patterns change.
Business value: Working capital decisions are backed by live AR data with expected impact quantified before any action is taken.
Dispute Resolution Agent
Scans incoming payments for deduction patterns, classifies each claim by type, retrieves supporting documentation from across the system landscape and routes a pre-built case file to the responsible team with a recommended resolution path. Stalled cases are escalated automatically.
Business value: Resolution cycle cut from weeks to days. At 2-4% of sales, systematic case handling delivers measurable, trackable revenue recovery.
